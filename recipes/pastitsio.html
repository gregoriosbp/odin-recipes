Pastitsio

Description

The Greek version of lasagna, pastitsio is an all-time Greek classic. Using thick tube-noodles, ground beef, and plenty of bechamel, this dish will fill you up and lay you out harder than a Thanksgiving smorgasbord. With a bit of love and care, this dish is a huge hit at any dinner party, potluck, or even for your family at home!

Serves between 4-6 people

Ingredients

  • Feta
  • Greek Buccatini
  • 1 Cinnamon Stick
  • Clove
  • Bay Leaf
  • Garlic
  • Tomato Paste
  • Can of Crushed Tomatoes
  • Red Onion
  • Red Wine
  • Ground Beef
  • Unsalted Butter
  • Kefalotyri Greek Cheese
  • Nutmeg
  • Milk
  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Panko Bread Crumbs

Steps

Pasta

  1. Boil pastitsio buccatini til al-dente
  2. Cut feta in small chunks and toss with pasta

Meat Sauce

  1. Cook down ground beef until browned
  2. Add 1 cup of red wine and reduce
  3. Add tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, clove, 1 bay leaf, 3 diced garlic cloves, and 1 cinnamon stick
  4. Let simmer, then allow to cool before assembly

Bechamel

  1. Add flour and butter to a pot then slowly incorporate milk into mixture by constant mixing
  2. Once thick enough, grate in kefalotyri and add nutmeg and mix until uniformly incorporated
  3. Add the yolk of two eggs and mix to incorporate

Assembly

  1. In a large casserole pan, add pasta to the base all running in the same direction
  2. Layer cooled meat sauce over top of the pasta and tamp flat across
  3. Pour bechamel over the meat sauce and spread across
  4. Grate kefalotyri across the surface of the bechamel and lightly coat surface area with panko
  5. Bake until top of pastitsio is golden brown
